Preparedness

Earthquake

An earthquake is a shaking of the earth’s upper crust caused by the sudden movement of a section of rock in the earth’s inner crust. When a section of this rock suddenly moves and causes a slippage of the adjacent section of rock, the energy created causes vibrations called seismic waves. These waves radiate up to the top of the earth’s crust, causing the earth to move violently.

Every inch of the earth’s surface is susceptible to earthquakes with some areas being more susceptible than others. If you live in one of those earthquake prone areas, you need to take precautions such as securing loose items in your home to include: Bolting or screwing items to the walls. Placing larger or heavier objects closer to the floor rather than having them on higher shelves. Bracing overhead light fixtures and installing flexible gas/water pipe and fittings. Repairing any electrical wiring or gas/water leaks, install automatic shut-off valves. Strapping down any movable larger objects which may slide or roll away such as a refrigerator or a stove. Ensuring that your home is securely fasten to its foundation. The most important thing to do is to be prepared by identifying the safest spot in your home and securing yourself/family and performing scheduled family earthquake drills.

If indoors, get yourself and your family to your chosen ‘safe spot’ and drop, cover, and hold on. Ensure that you are covered with a protective material to avoid getting hurt from flying materials. Be sure that you are not under any objects which might fall on you. Do not attempt to exit and go outside while the shaking/tremors are still active. Understand that most injuries occur when frantic people try to run or try to leave a secure environment. Expect fire alarms, sprinkler systems, and similar warning systems will be activated, do not be alarmed. If outdoors move to a open, clear area staying calming and ensuring not to run. If in a moving vehicle, stay in the vehicle, avoid stopping near or under bridges, trees, overpasses, or utility wires. If trapped in debris, cover your mouth and face, and stay still.

If inside, make very sure that all shaking has stopped and it is safe to go outside. Expect aftershocks, expect to see mass destruction, expect additional damage from weakened structures. Find a stable structure and text ‘Shelter + your zip code’ to 43362, if cellular service is available. Stay away from damaged areas; return home only when authorities say it is safe. Be careful when ingesting food and water, you must make sure they are safe. Wear long sleeve shirts and long pants, sturdy shoes and wear protective gloves. Clean spilled medicine, bleaches, gasoline, or any cleaning liquids immediately. Use extreme care if driving, expect the worst such as traffic light outages. Inspect all utilities before turning them on. If you have any doubts about your utilities before you turn them on, have a professional on sight to handle this. If you have a chimney, be sure to inspect every inch as unnoticed damage could be the start of a fire.
